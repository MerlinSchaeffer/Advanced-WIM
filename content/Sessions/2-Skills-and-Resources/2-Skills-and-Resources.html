Readings: Heckman and Kautz (2012), Bourdieu (1986)

Lecture slides: Lecture 1

In this session we will read a classic of sociology, Bourdieu’s essay on the forms of capital. We will then compare this essay with recent work by Heckman and others on the importance of (non-)cognitive skills. How do these arguments compare to one another? is Bourdieu’s concept of cultural capital all encompassing or poorly defined and unspecific? Importantly, how do these theories help us to make sense of and explain inequality in Denmark and across the world?

References

Bourdieu, Pierre. 1986. “The Forms of Capital.” Pp. 241–58 in Handbook of Theory and Research for the Sociology of Education, edited by J. Richardson. Westport, CT: Greenwood.
Heckman, James J., and Tim Kautz. 2012. “Hard Evidence on Soft Skills.” Labour Economics 19(4):451–64.
