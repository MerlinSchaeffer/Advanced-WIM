Readings: Bourdieu (1986:15–21) and Heckman and Kautz (2012).

Presentations: Protsch and Solga (2015) and Jackson (2009).

Lecture slides: Lecture 1

In this session we will read a classic of sociology, Bourdieu’s essay on the forms of capital. We will focus on its part about cultural capital and compare this essay with recent work by Heckman and others on the importance of (non-)cognitive skills. How do these arguments compare to one another? is Bourdieu’s concept of cultural capital all encompassing or poorly defined and unspecific? Importantly, how do these theories help us to make sense of and explain inequality in Denmark and across the world?
