Readings: Gambetta (2009), and Spence (1973).

Presentation: Schaeffer, Höhne, and Teney (2016).

Lecture slides: Lecture 1

In this session we will read Diego Gambetta’s overview of signalling theory and it’s use in sociology. We also skim read Spence’s original article on the matter. As an application we will hear a presentation on a study I conducted with two colleagues on the signalling model of statistical discrimination. We will discuss which other applications of signalling theory we can come up with in class.

References

Gambetta, Diego. 2009. “Signalling.” Pp. 168–94 in The Oxford handbook of analytical sociology, Oxford handbooks, edited by P. Hedström and P. Bearman. Oxford ; New York: Oxford University Press.
Schaeffer, Merlin, Jutta Höhne, and Celine Teney. 2016. “Income Advantages of Poorly Qualified Immigrant Minorities: Why School Dropouts of Turkish Origin Earn More in Germany.” European Sociological Review 32(1):93–107. doi: 10.1093/esr/jcv091.
Spence, Michael. 1973. “Job Market Signaling*.” The Quarterly Journal of Economics 87(3):355–74. doi: 10.2307/1882010.
